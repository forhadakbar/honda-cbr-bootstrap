Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Usually, it is not the outside shell that deteriorates first. It is typically the lining and foam inside of the helmet. If the inside of your helmet starts to flake off into your hair or onto your shoulders, that is a good sign you need a helmet replacement.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride? What should i check?

Check that the wheels spin freely, with no play in the bearings, and that the steering tube likewise moves freely with no play. Give it a test ride. Make sure the brakes work OK and that the wheels don't rub as they turn. Try a few corners to make sure there are no odd handling issues.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike? Safety first!

Wearing a helmet that fits well every time you're on a bike helps protect your face, head, and brain if you fall down. That's why it's so important to wear your bike helmet whenever you are on a bike.
